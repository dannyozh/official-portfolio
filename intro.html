HTML is not the same as prose

  • by:Daniel

19 September 2019 was the day I officially began on my quest to become a software engineer. Walking into General Assembly's swanky, modern space at 79 Anson Road in Singapore, I was filled with hope that this was the right move towards a better and brighter career path. Armed with a respectable amount of creative capital - from being a writer and professional musician - and a burning love for technology, I perceived the art of coding as my next Everest.

That's as far as the wave of innocent optimism reached, unfortunately. Being so accustomed to read and synthesize information at break-neck speed, the best-practice of reading code line-by-line and letter-by-letter proved to be a foreign change of pace. The errors started flooding in, represented by a defeating cacophany of white pages, unclickable links and disoriented images.

The realization that HTML is simply not structured the same way as prose hit me hard. The old, comfortable structure of introduction, content and insight would have to make way for a disciplined logical approach unlike any other I'd ever encountered. Perhaps my greatest takeaway from that day was the notion as code as an indivisible whole - every line plays a role in how your final 'dish' turns out. Intriguing stuff, though there was much to learn on the long road up ahead.
